House Rules & Code of Conduct

The rules below are the rules the club scoreboard enforces. The scorer enters each dart as thrown; the board does the arithmetic.

General Play

  • Each turn is three darts, thrown in order. A dart that misses the board is entered as a Miss.
  • Games need a minimum of 2 players and take a maximum of 12.
  • In X:001, playing order is the order players are added to the board. In Killer and Shanghai, the match is played in the order numbers were claimed.
  • Places. Every game (X:001, Killer, Shanghai) has a Places setting, 1 to 5, chosen before the game — default 2. Players outside the chosen places stay unplaced and are eligible for the Bonus Round.
  • The scorer may undo the last dart or clear the whole turn if a dart was entered wrong — fix it before the turn is ended.
  • The scoreboard's record is the record. The full match record can be downloaded as a Word document at the end of every game.

X:001 — 101 / 201 / 301

Every player starts on the chosen total (101, 201 or 301) and races to exactly zero.

Starting rule

  • Straight In Default. Every dart counts from the first throw.
  • Double In If selected before the game, a player's score doesn't start counting until they hit a double (any double, or the 50 bull).

Finishing

  • You must finish exactly on zero, and the dart that takes you to zero must be a double. The inner bull (50) counts as a double; the outer bull (25) does not.
  • Places. Before the game, choose how many finishing places are played for (1 to 5, default 2). With 1 place the game ends when someone finishes — a classic single winner. With more, finished players drop out of the rotation and play continues for Second, Third and so on until all chosen places are filled. If only one player is left unplaced, they take the final place automatically.

Busts

A turn is a bust — and the score goes back to what it was at the start of the turn — if any of these happen:

  • Below zero The turn takes the score under 0.
  • Score of 1 The turn leaves the score on exactly 1 (no double can finish from 1).
  • No double The score reaches 0 but the final dart wasn't a double.

Killer

  1. Claim a number. Before play starts, each player throws with their non-dominant hand until a dart lands on a free number — however many darts it takes. That number is theirs. Every player's number is unique — numbers 1–20 and both bulls are up for grabs, first in first served.
  2. Claiming order. Players claim in whatever order the club decides on the night — lucky pick, first to arrive, whatever. The match is then played in the order numbers were claimed: first to claim throws first.
  3. Attack. Hitting an opponent's number gives that opponent strikes: a single is 1 strike, a double is 2, a triple is 3. Either bull is 1 strike.
  4. Defend. Hitting your own number removes that many strikes from yourself (never below zero).
  5. Elimination. A player who reaches 5 strikes is eliminated. Hits on an eliminated player's number count for nothing.
  6. Winning. Last player standing wins.
  7. Places. Places (1 to 5, default 2) are awarded in reverse elimination order: the winner is First, the last player eliminated is Second, the one before them Third, and so on. Only the top places (by the number chosen) get an official rank — everyone else stays unplaced and can go into the Bonus Round.

Shanghai

  1. Claim a number. Before play starts, each player throws with their non-dominant hand until a dart lands on a free number from 1–20 — however many darts it takes; nobody's counting (except when it's Gary). Claiming is the only non-dominant throwing of the match; everything after is dominant hand. That number is theirs for the whole game. Bulls can't be claimed — there's no triple bull.
  2. Claiming order. Players claim in whatever order the club decides on the night — lucky pick, first to arrive, whatever. The match is then played in the order numbers were claimed: first to claim throws first.
  3. Make your Shanghai. Hit a single, a double and a triple of your own number — in any order, over as many turns as it takes. Three darts a turn; darts that land anywhere else count for nothing.
  4. Placings. Players are ranked in the order they complete all three. Choose how many places are played for before the game (1 to 5, default 2) — the game ends once all chosen places are taken, or when everyone has finished. If only one player is left unplaced, they take the next place automatically.

Bonus Round — Pint Shootout

When a game ends with players left over who didn't take a place, they can throw off for a pint. The scorer picks the format on the night from whatever the board's set up for:

  • Highest 3 Darts Every non-finalist throws three darts; highest total takes the pint. A tie means the tied players throw again — sudden death on the total.
  • Bull Shootout One dart each at the bull. A hit keeps you in; a miss puts you out. If everyone misses in a round, everyone survives and throws again. Last one standing wins the pint.
  • Full Game The non-finalists play a full X:001, Killer or Shanghai game between themselves, same rules as above, for the pint.

Purely for a pint on the night — the bonus round doesn't count toward the club ladder.

Code of Conduct

We play in the front bar of the Bellbrook Hotel. We're guests there, and the club only works if everyone can enjoy the night.

  • Respect the pub. Look after the board, the oche and the furniture. Treat hotel staff and other patrons with courtesy — they were here first.
  • Respect the throw. Stand clear and stay quiet while a player is at the line. No coaching, sledging or movement in their eyeline mid-throw. Banter belongs between turns.
  • The scorer's word is final. Query a score before the turn is ended, not three rounds later. Genuine disputes are settled by the players on the night, with a majority of those present having the final say.
  • Play what you hit. Call your darts honestly. Nobody's here for sharpshooting trophies — a club that trusts its scorers doesn't need an umpire.
  • Win and lose the same way. Shake hands (or tap knuckles) after every game. Celebrate wins without rubbing them in; cop losses without blaming the board, the lighting or the beer.
  • Drink sensibly. It's a pub game, not a drinking game. Anyone too far gone to throw safely sits out — the darts are sharp and the walls have suffered enough.
  • Everyone gets a game. New players, visitors and juniors (with a parent or guardian) are always welcome, and nobody gets left off the board on a club night.
  • Leave it better. Darts pulled, board straightened, glasses back to the bar at the end of the night.

Repeated or serious breaches are a matter for the club members present on the night, and the hotel's own house rules always take precedence.
